# Lab diary: 15 June 2026

> In collaboration with ChatGPT

Today I implemented initial concentration field (CF) plotting for the trajectory analysis. I added a new script for producing monthly CF anomaly maps for selected DMPS particle-size bins. The script loads the trajectory and concentration data, removes missing entries, matches concentration values to trajectory arrival times, and computes monthly grid-cell concentration fields.

For the first version, each trajectory contributes its matched concentration value to all grid cells visited by that trajectory. I then compute the grid-cell average concentration and express it as an anomaly relative to the geometric mean concentration over the available measurement period. I also added fixed anomaly color limits from −1 to +1 so that the plots are comparable across months.

Preliminary plots for particle sizes near 25 nm and 100 nm show plausible size-dependent transport patterns. The 100 nm plots resemble the expected accumulation-mode behavior more clearly, while the 25 nm plots show more influence from northwestern directions but are also noisier. The results are broadly encouraging, although the dataset only covers about two years of concentration measurements, so individual events may have a stronger influence than in the reference study.

So far, no concentration smoothing. This needs to be implemented next.
